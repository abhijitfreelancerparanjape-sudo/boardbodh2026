# Physics Paper Sourcing — CBSE + Maharashtra, Std 12, last 10 years

Where to get the raw material to upload, and the IP position on each. Everything below is for Physics first; the same sources serve Chemistry, Maths, Biology when you clone.

---

## The one-line answer

Questions are free from official sources for both boards. CBSE also gives official answers (marking schemes) free. Maharashtra gives questions only, no official answer key — so you generate Maharashtra model answers yourself with Claude. Nothing needs to be bought.

---

## CBSE Class 12 Physics

**Questions + official answers + more: free, official.**
- Source: CBSE academic site (cbseacademic.nic.in) and cbse.nic.in.
- Question papers: official "Previous Years' Question Papers" page (recent main + compartment papers).
- Marking schemes: separate official "Marking Scheme" page — this is CBSE's own model answer, the gold.
- Model Answer by Candidate: official page with actual topper-style answer scripts (XII PCMB where available). Strong reference signal for how a full-mark answer reads.
- Question banks + Additional Practice Questions: official CBSE Academic pages, per subject, with marking schemes — more pattern signal for generation.
- Sample papers + marking schemes: ~11 sessions (2015-16 to 2025-26).
- NCERT Class 12 Physics textbooks (Part I + II): free PDFs from ncert.nic.in.

## Maharashtra HSC (MSBSHSE) Class 12 Physics

**Questions + chapter-wise question banks: free, official. No official marking scheme/model answer.**
- Question papers: MSBSHSE official question paper page (mahahsscboard.in). Public archive is thinner than CBSE; treat the official site as source of record, third-party copies for discovery only.
- MSCERT / MAA question banks (maa.ac.in): official, chapter-wise and question-type organised. Very useful for the curriculum/concept map because they are already structured by chapter and question type. Physics is HSC subject code 54.
- Balbharati / eBalbharati Class 12 Physics textbook: free PDF.
- Important: the board does NOT publish a CBSE-like marking scheme or model-answer archive. So we generate Maharashtra Physics model answers (as rubric components) with Claude from the official questions.

## IP note (from Dhananjay's research)
Publicly available does NOT mean public domain. CBSE papers are CBSE's copyright (Rupendra Kashyap v. Jiwan Publishing House); NCERT and Balbharati content is copyrighted (Balbharati has a formal licensing policy). Facts, concepts, formulae, theorems and required scientific keywords are free to use. Protected expression (official wording, examples, diagrams, answer structure, point sequence) is not. So: use official material for metadata, chapter mapping, marks/difficulty tagging, and required-keyword lists; build original questions and original rubric components; link to official PDFs rather than hosting them; do not bulk-republish or line-by-line paraphrase. This is exactly why the schema separates `keywords` (free facts) from rubric component prose (our original expression).

## What NOT to use as a source

Third-party aggregators (Vedantu, Shiksha, Selfstudys, GradeHunt, maharashtraboardsolutions, etc.) are convenient and good for discovering what exists and sanity-checking patterns. But their compiled solutions are copyrighted. Do not ingest their answers or compilations into the corpus — that reintroduces exactly the private-IP risk the strategy avoids.

## Upload checklist for the MVP (Physics)

1. CBSE: download QP + MS for each year/set, last 10 years.
2. NCERT: download Class 12 Physics textbook PDFs.
3. Maharashtra: download official QP for each year, last 10 years.
4. Tag every source paper by chapter and micro-concept.
5. CBSE answers come from the marking scheme; Maharashtra answers get generated by Claude and reviewed.
6. Past papers are pattern reference only — the platform serves original board-style questions, not verbatim reproductions.

## Verify before relying on it
Source URLs and section layouts on government sites change. Confirm the exact download paths on cbseacademic.nic.in, ncert.nic.in, and the MSBSHSE site at the time you pull, rather than assuming a fixed link.
